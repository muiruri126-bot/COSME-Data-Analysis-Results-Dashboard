A. HOUSEHOLD CHARACTERISTICS - MEN'S PERSPECTIVE

Male Household Leadership Dynamics

Male-headed households: 67.3% ‚Üí 71.4%, showing continued patriarchal structure but increasing engagement. Men's education stable at 42.1% primary level. Key finding: Male participation in household surveys increased 38.5% ‚Üí 61.2%, indicating growing openness to gender dialogue and willingness to engage in household decisions. Mixed households with shared leadership now at 28.6% (up from 18.9%), suggesting norm shifts toward more collaborative approaches.

Location Distribution
Marine sites more engaged: 64.2% of male respondents from marine areas show higher conservation awareness.
Household Headship
Male-headed households stable, but male awareness of shared responsibilities increased significantly.
Primary Income Activities
Men's primary income: Agriculture 52.8%, Small business 24.1%, Casual labor 18.3%. Emerging shift toward enterprise over manual labor.
Housing Materials
Natural floor materials 58.3% ‚Üí 61.7%, reflecting modest vulnerability. Men's housing priorities focus on agricultural structures.
WASH Access
Water access 81.5% ‚Üí 76.9% (drought impact). Toilet facilities 68.4% ‚Üí 71.2%, showing men's sanitation investment increasing.
B. SHOCKS, STRESSES & COPING STRATEGIES

Male Response to Climate Shocks

Men's shock exposure: Drought 71.2% ‚Üí 64.8%, Heat waves 6.3% ‚Üí 19.7%, Flooding 3.1% ‚Üí 8.5%. Key insight: Men reporting "very large" shock impact decreased 28.5% ‚Üí 16.3%, demonstrating improved household resilience. Male coping strategies shifted toward collaborative approaches: selling livestock decreased 22.1% ‚Üí 14.5%, while formal loans increased 8.3% ‚Üí 31.2%. Evidence of program-supported behavioral adaptation in crisis response.

Types of Climate Shocks
Heat waves emerging stress for men (6.3% ‚Üí 19.7%) requiring adaptation strategies.
Shock Impact Assessment
Positive: Men reporting large/very large impacts declining, suggesting better adaptation capacity.
Coping Strategies
Loans increased 8.3% ‚Üí 31.2% (financial access), Savings 7.1% ‚Üí 12.4%. Men adopting formal mechanisms over distress sales.

Critical shift: Men now seen as preparedness coordinators in households and communities, with 67.3% reporting they discuss disaster plans with family members (up from 18.5%). Male leadership in DRR creating cascading household protection effects.

E. SAVINGS, LOANS & FINANCIAL SERVICES

Men's Financial Behavior Transformation

Men personally saving: 31.4% ‚Üí 68.2% (+36.8pp). Men in savings groups: 18.5% ‚Üí 42.7%, but critical shift: 84.3% now saving through wives' VSLAs (delegation/trust increase). Men taking loans: 22.8% ‚Üí 58.6%. Loan purpose shift dramatic: agricultural input loans 78.3% ‚Üí 42.1%, while household/women loans 8.5% ‚Üí 48.2%. Savings use transformed: large assets 31.2% ‚Üí 18.6%, agriculture 28.4% ‚Üí 22.7%, household equipment 12.5% ‚Üí 38.4%. Evidence of values shift from accumulation to household welfare and women's economic activities.

Personal Savings Participation
Men saving rate nearly doubled (31.4% ‚Üí 68.2%), indicating financial behavior change.
Savings Mechanisms
VSLA participation 18.5% ‚Üí 42.7%. Men increasingly trusting group savings (84.3% household savings via wife).
Loan Taking Behavior
Loan access expanded 22.8% ‚Üí 58.6%. Men's credit profile strengthened.
Savings Use Priorities
Dramatic shift from asset accumulation to household welfare and women's business support.
